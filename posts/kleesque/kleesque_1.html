Grid Modern

My infatuation with the grid continues. This time I was inspired to explore impressions whispered by Mondrion and Klee. These whispers quickly drifted away and I was subsumed in the technical task of manageing code, seeing where it would take me as I talked with it.

Code is the medium. But I like to thing that the resulting artwork is the application which continues generating variations on sets of parameters. In the reductionsist sense, I am trying to explore space, color, movement, gesture through the expansion of the grid through the introduction of random arrangement, that is, playing with the strictures of the two dimensional plane.

To give an idea of what generative art means for me, I had the thought this morning that these art pieces will continu forming and becoming and revealing themselves as long as they are hosted on a computer somewhere. They present versions of infinity. In that sense they are pieces of infinite artwork.

If that sounds a little pompous, I appoloze. It actually feels humbling to me. After all, the grid, and the programing language, and the computers are just media and concepts that I am borrowing and playing with for a brief window of time.
